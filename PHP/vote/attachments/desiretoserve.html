THE DESIRE TO SERVE

                                                            Jesse D. Alto

 

            One of the most important and shall we say godlike quality of man is his desire to serve. Man and woman become husband and wife not only because they love each other  and promise to be together till death they part (so some say), but because they deeply want to serve and care for one another. Parents protect and serve their children until they become independent and learn how to take care of themselves. Then too the children resolve to serve and to take care of their parents old age come. The family is the simplest unit where the desire to serve is best exemplified. And to serve mankind comes in different forms and of inclusive effect. While serving others they serve themselves.  Farmers till the soil not only to earn a living for his family but primarily that non-farmers may have foods to eat. Teachers devotedly teach the young to be in the future better citizens and leaders of  nation. Builders and carpenters construct building and home for the shelter of others. Food and product manufacturers produce products for other to use that make life easier and worth living. Doctors cure the sick, religious leaders preach the teachings of holy books to strengthen the faith of men that they truly reflect the image of God. Many political leaders want to lead a nation with resolved to attain their goal most effectively and efficiently.

 

The desire to serve his nation and the people is driven by various forms of motivation often laced with personal and selfish interest and less often selflessly. In fact, a television commentator has said that this 2001 election has shown very unique qualities both from the voters and the candidates alike. Voters and candidates are emotionally charged. Voters actively participated in the selection process of candidates that will be elected as public servants. Before and after the election, voters want those elected public officials to provide them services of varying interest ranging from material benefit, emotional, and mental satisfaction. Voters realize the value of their vote and are now demanding more from the candidates the expected services from them once elected.  Voters are looking deeper into the political platform, qualifications, credentials, integrity, and competence of candidates who they will vote for. And those young voters who failed to vote because of their failure to register as new voters participated in the selection process of candidates to make the election clean and honest.

 

            In this 2001 election, millions of pesos were spent by candidates to get  elected. Money is used to buy votes and corrupt the voting people in their quest for position and power which aggravated and lowered the moral standard of the nation. The money being spent is way above the expected salaries that they will earn should the candidates be elected into office. This is highly anomalous unless the candidate is rich and willing to spend his fortune to serve unselfishly his nation. Or the candidate is highly qualified to the position aspired for and many of his supporters are willing to spend their own money as donation to help the candidate elected.  Or some candidates are determined to get themselves into office for power, name, and fame. So before the conclusion of this election process and even before the final vote is counted and final results determined, several contending political parties are in fact accusing each other of vote-buying and other frauds just to get their candidates elected. Even the officials in charge of conducting the election process are being accused of favoring certain candidates to win. Certainly the desire to be elected is more than just to serve the nation but also to satisfy personal and selfish interest of some candidates. Some candidates are accused of using money, gun, and other forms of frauds to get themselves elected. This may not be true to all candidates, but serving the people and nation through the elected offices now is   a ploy to earn political power for selfish interest. Why is desire and service so strong to compel leaders to act as they do?  Let us understand what is desire and service.

 

DESIRE

 

            Desire is a wish or long for, a fuel, a force that compels or motivates one to attain a chosen goal. Human desire is expressed request, a petition. It is an appetite, a passion, a want or craving. Desire may be selfish or selfless. Desire is selfish or greed when one is acquisitive, hateful, and motivated to act for the satisfaction of his personality. Desire is selfless when his action is impersonal, kind, compassionate resulting to the common good of all. It compels him to act to benefit all. Selfless desire is love, vision, aspiration distinct only to a great or holy man.

 

 

SERVICE

 

            Service is an act of devotion to another, or an  act to benefit others. Service is a favor.  It is an act of assistance to benefit other. It is altruism or godlike quality of man. Good leaders use feeling and understanding to be able to serve best those in want.  Good leaders know that giving fish is not the permanent solution to alleviate hunger, but   teaching the poor and the hungry how to fish by themselves and become totally self reliant.  In the deeper sense, service is that develops man’s full creative faculties and become independent and self-reliant that promotes peace, harmony and prosperity to the nation that he led.  In other words, it is the elimination of his dependence to the nation for personal and material needs. It is putting the leadership of the nation into the hand of competent, honest, and dedicated leaders who are tasked to manage the affairs of the government promoting peace, freedom, prosperity, and most of all the highest moral dignity of men. It is the realization that man must cooperate with all and eliminate the sense of separateness, a distinct individuality between rich and poor in thought, feeling, and deeds which qualities are the root and cause of all evil. A good leader is one who realizes his unity with all and serves the people to be self reliant in thoughts, words, and deeds. A good leader realizes that each and every person has the potentialities to create something that contribute to the wealth, progress, and peace of the nation provided they are given the opportunity to live free of hunger and in health and is given the opportunity of education and training necessary to develop his skills and talent. Leader and follower act harmoniously. They act in accordance with the universal scheme and law; and harmony in consciousness and thought and therefore in action is what men understand by the term ethics. Ethics are not a convention; morals are not a convention; they are rooted in the harmony, in the central laws of being; they are based on the very structural harmony of the universe.

 

            But like a dirt that fell in the glass of clean water and so spoil the water, greedy political leaders use money and superficial act of service to deceive many into voting him into office for deeper selfish and personal interest. Graft and corruption prevail and moral degeneration follows the nation. Politicians who cheat and buy votes  to win an election are selfish and cannot be expected to lead and manage honestly and dedicatedly expected on the position aspired for. Those who won an election by cheating cannot be expected to implement the laws of the land since those who have seen his examples cheating to win an election cannot be morally persuaded to obey the laws wholeheartedly. Manmade laws will be written without effective implementation.

 

CONCLUSION

 

            The desire to serve is inherent to all men regardless one is at the lowest economic ladder of the society or at the highest.  The performance of service is a happy or blissful an act  from the offering of water to the thirsty, a flower to a love one, a kind and loving smile to a stranger, and a gift or offering to some one care for so long as the action is wholeheartedly given and without attachment or expectation of return. Obeying traffic rules, placing garbage to designated container/place, being polite and respecting freedom and rights of other as he seeks rights and freedom for himself, these and many more are services all including the masses can serve the nation without cost and at ease and with happiness. These are the kind of services all can give freely and most precious and abundantly without cost to themselves and will save the government the cost of manpower services to realize savings for other more important services. This is the kind of services that bring smile and comfort not only the receiver but equally to the giver. All, from the leader to the masses can give these services that certainly bring comfort and smile to the receivers. These are the kind of services that bring peace and happiness to all.

 

            But one must be wary to the services offered by politicians who are motivated to gain power, name and fame for selfish interest. Gift and services offered in exchange for votes often lead later to graft and corruption. All must work together to make our leaders lead the nation to prosperity, self reliant, dignity and high moral qualities of the nation as a whole. Leaders must implement the laws of the land equal to all and without discrimination. The intellectual competence and moral ascendancy of the leader is measured by his own compliance of the laws of the land, and not by his making them only for the benefit of the few. He is a superior leader when he can lead by examples obeying and implementing the laws of the land than by making new laws and shelves them gathering dust in the court of justice. Leaders are elected to serve the people and the nation, not themselves.
