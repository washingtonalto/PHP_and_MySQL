FREEDOM, POWER AND RESPONSIBILITY

Jesse D. Alto

 

 

Following the rule of succession as expressed by the Philippine constitution, President Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo has succeeded as President of the Republic of the Philippines when former President Joseph Ejercito Estrada left Malacañang Palace on January 20, 2001. During the impeachment trial against former President Joseph Estrada the prosecutors were forced to leave the impeachment trial and protested because eleven senators-judges prevented the opening of the second envelope containing vital evidences supporting the charges against the former president.  The charges against the former president showed that he enriched himself of billions of pesos by various illegal means. People throughout the Philippines rallied and demanded the resignation of the former president. The people demanded that the wheel of justice to prevail on the various corruption and plunder charges against the former president. The suppression to reveal vital and important documents against the various charges against former President Joseph Ejercito Estrada inflamed and angered many. The Supreme Court recognized the vacancy in the office of the president and affirmed the legitimacy of President Arroyo’s ascension to the highest office of the land.

 

            After former President Joseph Ejercito Estrada is charged of plunder and is put to jail without bail, his supporters started to rally behind him and within few days sizable number of his supporters gathered and stayed at the Edsa Shrine. Then on May 1, 2001, the crowd supporting the former president walked and went reaching the gate of the Malacañang Palace demanding his release and allowed him to return to his former position. Some died and many were hurt from the side of those demanding the return of the former president to Malacañang and from the police and military personnel maintaining peace and order given the very volatile situation prevailing at that moment. Millions worth of properties were damaged and lost because of the riot. Those supporting the former president want him to be back to his former office in due respect to the l0 millions voters who placed him to the position. On the other hand, President Arroyo explained that the legal cases of the former president is not political but that the rule of law on the many cases he is charged are heard in court. Former President Estrada is given all his rights to defend and prove his innocence. President Arroyo also explained that a few politicians want to create chaos and destabilize the government for selfish political interest using the masses to promote their evil end. She declared State of Rebellion in Metro Manila to bring back the rule of law and peace and to justice those responsible for the illegal acts and the destruction of properties.

 

Several approaches are available to shed lights from the events just mentioned.  Let the political, legal, and economic luminaries elaborate the points within their skills and mastery. Let us limit our study and understanding on the part of human nature in his continuing quest for freedom and power as the means for peace and happiness. It is also the feeling and thought by man to express his creative power and freedom often selfishly and at times selflessly. There is also that varying sense of responsibility by those who exercise vast amount of power and freedom. It is the divine inheritance of every man to exercise power and freedom that he may enjoy the fruit of his action so long as an equivalent amount of responsibility are also applied to promote unity in diversity. The exercise of power and freedom must be tempered by responsibility resulting there from.  Some cannot understand the meanings of freedom, power, and responsibility. But some crafty and selfish politicians want power and freedom for selfish interest. Let us try to understand them:

 

FREEDOM

 

            Freedom is a condition of being free of restraints.  To act without restraints is freedom and therefore joy. Freedom to love one’s country is guaranteed by our constitution. In the national anthem, we sing that one is free to love and die for the country.  Freedom is expression of joy and will inherent of the right of each individual. It is in freedom that one experiences the beauty of life and the joy of living. But freedom to act is not absolute. The act must be tempered with responsibility assumed by the one acting. Freedom to act must be tempered by the natural laws and the man made laws of the land. Action taken beyond the bound of the natural laws and the laws of the land are meted with corresponding negative or painful consequences. Freedom to act as he chooses is inherent and right of all men. But action has to be measured by an equal amount of assumed responsibility so that all can live peacefully in diversity. Action within the bound of responsibility or morality makes man upright and virtues. Action to serve own interest only and at the expense of others is selfish and immoral. As the joyful bird uses his wings to fly in freedom up in the sky so a joyful man is free expressing his capacities and power within the bound of his responsibilities to nature and mankind alike.

 

POWER

 

            Power is strength or force exerted. Man possesses physical, emotional, and mental power in order to gain knowledge, experience, joy or suffering in life. An athlete will train his body to gain physical power and excel in the game of his choice. Man will study and work to gain emotional satisfaction and recognition. Man will study to gain knowledge that is power. Politicians want elected to gain power to serve the people or for selfish end. Wealth is power to do what one wishes to gain fame, recognition and honor. There is also that power of speech that makes man leader of his race or nation. But power is like wine that intoxicates man to greed, selfishness, and immorality. A drunken man of power serves only himself at the expense of his people.

 

RESPONSIBILITY

 

            Responsibility is a state, a quality, or fact of  being responsible.  Responsibility is acceptance of duty, burden or obligation. It is a ring-pass-not that define action to be within bound. It is the compass in the sea of life one is to have to reach his quest and destination. The recognition of responsibility is noticeable in varying degrees among men regardless of their material position and educational training in life. Responsibility is the bound or field which defines right action. The greater or broader the field of action, the greater is the responsibility to be assumed.  Responsibility is a moral obligation assumed by one taking action regardless one is a leader or a follower.

 

THE MASSES

 

            The interplay of forces used by some leaders among the masses who played significant role in the gathering at the Edsa shrine resulted the following:

 

1.                  The masses voice up and presented valid complaints about their need for freedom from wants (food, shelter, and peace).

2.                  The masses voice up the lack of education and security for the future of their children.

3.                  The seeking of justice on apparent injustices committed by government leaders.

4.                  The apparent misapplication of the justice systems. The powerful and the moneyed can get away with their crimes, but not the poor. In other words, powerful leaders refuse to accept greater responsibility in their decision and action in leading the nation. Selfishness prevails over service in running the affairs of the government.

5.                  A demand for justice to a fallen leader.

 

It is clear that the masses are not only neglected by some selfish politicians but are also being used by the politicians for their selfish interest. The masses want freedom to express their potentialities and creativity. They want the government to help them alleviate the burden in the maintenance of body and health. They seek assistance from the government to provide them work that they may enjoy freedom, develop power and assume broader responsibilities as human beings to a greater height. Better education and social services are requested that the masses are given opportunities for self-reliance and eventually become better citizens of the nation.

 

THE LEADERS

 

From the events that unfolded, many leaders wanted power but not the equal responsibilities that go with the aspired power and position. Many are not willing to assume accountabilities and responsibilities for action committed. To many of them, the end justifies the means.

 

1.                  There are leaders who want power, name, and fame. They even use the masses by apparently providing them with petty material needs and allowing them to stay in illegally constructed shanties in private and public land as squatters to insure votes from the masses come election time.

2.                  There are leaders who use money, guns, and prohibitive drugs to keep themselves in power. They engage themselves in illegal sources of income to be able to finance election campaign and to be able to provide petty financial assistance to the poor in exchange for votes and loyalty.

3.                  There are leaders willing to destroy others to assure them in power.

4.                  These leaders as soon as elected want to regain money spent during the election campaign by engaging themselves in illegal or get-rich fast activities. The nation and people suffer.

 

On the other hand, there are leaders willing to assume greater responsibilities and power to protect and uphold the laws of the land. They are the enlightened leaders who are seeking the position of power to help and serve the people. They seek position to serve the people, not to enrich themselves and certainly not for selfish interest.

 

CONCLUSION

 

            The government leaders must prioritize the educational and social programs designed to alleviate the physical, emotional and mental suffering of the poor. They should be trained to be self reliant and productive partners in the total economic programs of the nation. Each person must be made aware of the greater potentialities as a person to live in freedom, acquired but properly tempered power and guided by high moral responsibilities.

 

            The leaders in government must be chosen based on their platform of government, credential and trainings for the position aspired for and most of all for the one pointed will and dedication to serve the people and nation. Let us avoid voting for a leader whose selfish interest prevails over the need of his constituents.
