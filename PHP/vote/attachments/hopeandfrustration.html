HOPE AND FRUSTRATION

Jesse D. Alto

 

 

From street cleaner to the highest leader of a company, from religious leaders to political leaders, from farmers, law enforcers, business and legal professionals and media have hopes and frustrations in what are happening to our country today. Job seekers cannot find jobs. Businessmen cannot collect on time their receivables from customers. Borrowers cannot pay long matured bank loans. Many business establishments have already closed shops and many more are closing. Exposes of various crimes are the favorites of newsmen as headlines of their papers. Witnesses to crimes are coming up to tell tales of truth or otherwise of their participation and the masterminds of the crimes. Politicians change from one party to another to suit their convenience and to favor their chance of getting elected next time around or landing to a higher and bigger power and authority to reach higher fame. Some commented that our country is deteriorating fast that it may be best to go elsewhere to live. Others say that Filipinos are of a unique breed and can overcome such problems as they overcome greater problems and difficulties of the past. Some wonder why Filipinos obey faithfully foreign laws while overseas but not so at home. Some say that we have a crab mentality and will not allow others to succeed and so will always remain at the bottom economically in relation to other countries. These and many more are the frustrations anyone can hear from all sectors of the society.

 

There is truth in all the above-mentioned observations and perceptions. In fact the two “People Power” rallies of the political and religious leaders with participation of the masses resulted in the successful change of political leadership twice in the past. The masses participated in the people power rallies because of the strong and vivid perception of the masses to correct wrongdoings committed by the political leaders. The global economic meltdown started in l998 and continuing to the present time also resulted to the reduction of global economic activities. This resulted in lower business activities particularly exports. This reduces the chance of finding gainful employment to many job seekers. This reduces income of the wage earners resulting in the increase of those living beyond the poverty level. This threat of economic slowdown not only affects the Philippines but also its neighboring countries, and including Europe and the United States of America.  These countries are making new economic plans and economic policies to arrest recession. For example, to arrest recession the Singaporean leader told his constituents to innovate and change the ways of doing business to adopt to the changing global economic needs. He asked the people to be self-reliant and review for possible improvement of the business plans and the usual activities which may be effective in the past but may not be so this time.

 

            The political leaders of the nation are pushing economic and political reforms and are implementing policies and economic activities to increase agricultural output, expand infrastructure activities like roads and housing projects for the masses and other projects like mass transport system and transportation of goods designed to arrest recession and to prepare the nation produce products that are competitive in the global market.  Foreign investors are invited to come and help the nation for the needed resources to actualize planned economic projects and increase business activities particularly in the food production and manufacturing sectors. Likewise, labor leaders are asking for higher wages to meet increasing cost to maintain household needs. Some foreign investors positively responded but several declined because of the perception that the nation is not safe to invest their money in and the danger of kidnapping and the corrupt government officials they are likely to ask bribes before getting the licensing approval and implementation of projects. In short, the Philippines while enjoying abundant natural resources and qualified manpower compared to that of Taiwan and Singapore, do not have higher degree of trust and confidence from possible investors to bring in needed capital and work for greater employment opportunities. Over a year ago, a report was published covering more than 90 countries worldwide from least to most corrupt nations, the Philippines is rank 65th which means that the nation belongs to the one-third most corrupt nations in the world. And so, many talented Filipinos cannot find good jobs and prefer to go elsewhere at the pain and risk of leaving their families here due to lack of business activities where they can be gainfully employed. Investors will come only if assured that they are entering business in a competitive but fair playing field without political influences leading to graft and corruption. Those few who know how to deal with corrupt officials may come but the price of exploitation far exceeds than the expected benefits to be earned.

 

            Let us list the hopes and frustrations from insight cited above:

 

HOPE

 

1.                  The Philippines has abundant natural resources for use in the business activities nationwide. Agriculture production and food processing are businesses, which can be put up with definite positive results. The strong political will to construct dams nationwide will assure ready supply of water for irrigation and electricity production, including needed potable water supply for the people. However, the indiscriminate cutting of trees and pollution generated by unsupervised business activities by the government may pose serious danger to people, land and natural resources and its habitation as well

2.                  For now, the Philippines has talented and literate and easy to train working group of people needed in the production of goods and services. There is risk though that this may not be sustained depending on the trust of the educational programs  to continue  improving the literacy in the technical, English, and moral development of the youth. The recent survey show that the Philippines education is now lagging behind that of its neighbors which will severely affect the nation’s people competence and literacy  in the long run . This is a danger sign that the Filipinos are showing sign of moral and intellectual degeneration and our political leaders must look into it seriously and take corrective steps soon.

3.                  We have talented professionals like doctors, engineers, bankers, accountants, and managers of various kind who are respected and recognized abroad which prove that the Philippines has human resources needed to actualize the economic growth of the nation.  The opportunities to come back here and work must be provided by the political leaders by changing the present state of shaky economic and political atmosphere to one that all investors can trust the government and its leaders to put up their stake here.

4.                  The people are resilient, dedicated, hard working, non-violent and fun loving. The two people power rallies which resulted in the change of political leadership without bloodshed proved this point. The millions of Filipino workers who are continuously being appreciated by their foreign employers also proved the competence and dedication to work of this working class. Without them, the Philippines may have suffered severely in the recent and still on-going economic meltdown.

5.                  The trained and talented millions of  Filipino workers here and overseas also proved that the Philippines has abundant manpower reserve if in case in the future the country succeeds in reversing the shaky economic trend to upturn economic activities later.

6.                  The increase in wages will be easily attained as soon as the commensurate productivity is reached. Filipino overseas who earn much higher wages yet produce cheaper products for their employers proved that with higher productivity the labor cost can be adjusted to the satisfaction of the workers. Discontent because of low wage is eliminated and so are workers stoppage and strike.

 

FRUSTRATION

 

            The distrust and frustration of the people in the government and the hesitation of investors to come in are due to the following:

 

1.                  Graft and corruption practiced by some government officials asking money and bribes before official functions and responsibilities  are performed.

2.                  Crimes and kidnapping are rampant mainly because of the lack of opportunity the legal  ways to earn a living. The use of illegal drugs is also growing into a serious problem as the result of growing discontent among the different sectors of the society.

3.                  Circumventing or not implementing the laws supposed to be used in the efficient and effective deterrent to crime violators..

4.                  The blatant violations of laws by those who are suppose to implement the laws. The violators are not caught and penalized for the committed violations. Recent events show that some of  those task to implement the laws are the ones violating it with impunity.

5.                  The use of legal and technical shield in the prevention of the prosecution of the crime committed with or without physical evidences create impression that only poor who cannot afford to pay talented lawyers go to jail. Crime is committed because he did it, not because there is no  physical proof  that he committed the crime. Without setting aside or ignoring serious indiscretion to justify penning the crime mainly on probable cause sufficient probability of causes in the commission of crime may be enough for conviction. Crime committed without concrete evidence left behind by the perpetuator is still a crime.

6.                  The use of man made laws to justify and legalize immoral and selfish interest of some groups and those in power. The examples are the conversion of purchases to buy things not budgeted on, using the funds of the government controlled insurance and social security systems to invest on improperly valued securities resulting in  huge losses to the corporations managed by government officials.

 

CONCLUSION

 

The above shows that the Filipinos and the nation have the materials, productive labors, and natural resources to produce and manufacture products for own consumption and for exports The use of modern technology in the efficient production of goods will make the costs of production cheaper and competitive in the world market. One of the serious problem besetting the nation is not technical since Filipinos are technically trained and well accepted and well-like by their employers at home and abroad. Investors are not coming to bring capital in and technical know how to produce quality, cheap, and highly marketable products for local needs and exports because of  distrust to the government economic and political stability, graft and corruption and crimes. Investors will be willing to invest in long term infrastructure projects if convinced that they are protected and also their investment is safe and will earn decent return of investment.  In short, the problems are economics, political and morality. The strong influence of other  investor nations to the Philippines to create Anti-Laundering law is just one example of the trust needed in doing business here. The leaders know good economic policies and make them work.  Intellectual leaders may be chosen to implement the economic and political policies. But morality problem has to be improved if we are to gain trust and confidence of those we do business with.

 

            The morality* of the people and their leaders must be transformed  from the present degenerated state of morality to one of trust, confidence, respect and cooperation.  The following steps may be considered to improve the morality of the nation:

 

1.                  Moral education must be taught in schools to the youth of the land. Dr. Jose Rizal said that one can easily guides a young tree into a straight and tall matured tree later, but not a crooked matured tree forced into straight tree without considerable effort if at all possible.

2.                  Exercise political will in implementing the laws of the land and penalize violators, more so on those violators who are supposed to uphold and implement the laws. Loyalty and love to the country begins and the selfish interest and those of friends and relatives end.

3.                  Elect political leaders who are intellectuals and have higher sense of  ethical and moral standards.  The political power exercise by such leader must be commensurate to his higher sense of responsibility**. The desire to serve the people is higher than his selfish interest.

4.                  Without proselytizing to prevent religious conflicts, the religious teachings on morality may be taught in schools to enhance higher ethical and moral standard of the youth.

5.                  Improve the income of the public servants commensurate to their authorities and responsibilities. But the public servants must exercise higher ethical and moral standard in the discharge of  their responsibilities.

6.                  The income of wage earners in the private sectors will automatically improve as soon as there is renewed economic activities particularly if the workers productivity is improved due to new production technology and investors confidence to the country is renewed.

 

 

            Once upon a time, there was a war-torn country troubled by warlords in its midst. Criminals abound and economy in disarray.  In one part of the country, a jail warden is assigned to reform the criminals. The jail warden succeeded in teaching the criminals good manners, ethics, and right conduct. He also taught them to love his family, respect the elders and pay proper courtesy to authority and obey the laws of the land. Soon the criminals became good citizens and assets to the country. That part of the country became peaceful and prospered economically because of  the newly found confidence of the people and trust to the state. Soon that state became the envy of their neighbors. The jail warden was Confucious and the country is China. Good ethical standard and higher sense of morality are the answer to our social and economic problems.

 

            Thirty seven (37) years ago, the newly independent Singapore had very serious economic and morality problems. Water was rationed and few owned home of their own. Transportation and business opportunities were scarse,  public phones were vandalized and inoperative, gang wars among the ethnic Chinese, Malays, and Indians residence worsen the security of the tiny island. With sound economic policies and effective political leadership, Singapore is transformed into  a leading financial and business leaders in the world today. Singaporean nationals coming from the Chinese, Indian, and Malays descents no longer fight one another but cooperate and work harmoniously together. The country is clean and all obey the laws of the land. Penalty and punishment are surely given to the offenders of the laws and peace and harmony prevail. Recently, the writer had the opportunity to travel to Singapore through the assistance of a travel agency. Everything went according to the travel plan and the trip was a success. However, the travel agency promised to give us a small souvenir which was not given. This was overheard by the driver who at that time was already bringing us on the way to the airport leaving Singapore in about three (3) hours.  Even beyond his responsibility, the driver told the travel agency the unfulfilled promise and within two hours while we were waiting at the airport, the small souvenir came with apology. The driver said that a promise must be fulfilled and as a Singaporean, it is his responsibility to protect the dignity of his nation. Trust and confidence to a nation is built by this small event in daily life. We have many examples of this kind from our honest Filipino taxi drivers who return valuables to the rightful owners. Can we expect from our leaders of the land to do the same?
