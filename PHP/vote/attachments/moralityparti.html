MORALITY, A SERIOUS PROBLEM OF THE NATION

Jesse D. Alto

 

            Because of politics, economics, and moral issues, two main groups are hotly arguing   Philippines President  Joseph Ejercito Estrada  is removed from office through resignation or  impeachment. This is the first time in the history of the nation that the President is impeached.  Because of this the two main protagonists are working hard to win the heart and mind of the senators who will judge the President guilty or not, as charged. The whole nation and the world will be watching the proceeding with mixed emotion and anxiety awaiting the outcome. While the trial is going on, the Philippine economy is showing sign of cracking primarily because of the business leaders   want to see  the final outcome before implementing their business plan  either to reduce or increase business activities.  Foreign investors are also wary to continue their business presence in the country and trust the present administration.

 

            The two political camps are doing all they can using money and political resources to be on top and become the dominant political power to rule the country. The opposition expects to manage the political affairs of the government if the President is ousted. On the other hand, President Estrada wants to stay in power until 2004, his six year term of office. The opposition wants President Estrada out justifying that the four impeachment charges will make him unfit to govern.  On the other hand, the lawyers of  the President are using all the legal tricks and scheme to retain and advance his political interest. Seemingly, they use all means to justify ends.

 

            The economy is showing sign of slowing down and businessmen and people are worried.  If the economy is affected, there will be severe economic consequences which  bring about  business and production shutdown resulting to massive unemployment and spiraling costs of commodities.  Many are going to the streets to rally for the resignation or impeachment of  President Estrada.  Equally, the men of President Estrada are putting up  counter charges against the opposition. Seemingly, the power to govern is the issue at hand. But looking deeper into the problem and admitting that there is a need for strong leadership in government to arrest the breakdown in the political and economic stability of the nation, the more acute and real problem besetting the nation is a morality problem now facing the nation and its leaders.

 

            In the political front, it is evident that the high and the middle income groups , including the educated ones favor the resignation or impeachment of President  Joseph Estrada.  The business community  too is very much in favor for his ouster from the presidency. However, the poor and the uneducated which comprise the majority of the masses still favor the retention of the President to stay in office. Clearly, those who do not understand the consequences and the implication of  the charges of  alleged wrongdoings of the President  want him to continue in office. The President popularity as a hero in many of his leading role in his earlier movie career help much to maintain his popularity to the uneducated masses. But those who understand managing a nation and businesses do not favor him to hold on running the affairs of the government. As the result, the President is impeached on four counts as follow:

 

      • Culpable violation of the constitution
      • Bribery
      • Graft and corruption
      • Betrayal of public trust

 

            The Philippine Congress is now deliberating his impeachment cases and the results will be expected within few weeks.

 

            Why is morality  the  real problem besetting the nation? What is morality and its leadership significance?  A group is led by its own kind. Accountants, lawyers, doctors, educators and even thieves and robbers are led by their own kind.  There is truth that birds of the same feather flock together. The members of the group will select or elect a leader that best represent the skills, traits, character, vision and morality. Any  error committed in selecting and electing a leader in a society can be influenced by the form of government used. For example, it is true that in a democratic form of government any one can be elected irregardless of  intellect  and moral qualification.  A vote of one uneducated man is equal to the vote of  a brilliant and talented leader.  So the more uneducated persons there are in a nation, the more error in selecting a good leader is likely to happen. The society will suffer for the error committed but such error can be corrected. Such is the case now  where the President is impeached for alleged wrong doings. This seeming error can be understood as a question of morality since those who think that the President has no moral ascendancy to govern is invoking  such for his ouster.

 

A morally upright  leader  is one who possesses the following traits:

 

1.            He is compassionate and kind to his constituents.  He is harmless and is always looking for the goodness and welfare of all.

2.            He exercises truth and not falsehood. He uses this  moral quality  in all his action for fair play and governance.

3.            He does not steal and appropriate private and public properties for his selfish interest.

4.            He exercises temperance physically, emotionally and intellectually. A family is the simplest form of government and such temperance is required if peace and harmony is to prevail in the family. More so such quality is needed in governing a nation.

5.            A morally upright leader must not be covetous and selfish, particularly those that are not his own. He should allow everyone to prosper using  own resources and merit.  A leader should not exploit other for his  selfish interest.

6.            He exercises dicipline and restraints to rule fairly and honestly particularly on matters affecting the nation.

 

The above description of morality is supported by many. But  equally are those while supporting the above description of a morally upright leader do not practice what they believe in and instead committed action contrary to the above, and so engage in a seemingly double moral standard in life. The lack of proper education among the masses in the understanding of good manner and right conduct  now resulted to the lack of appreciation and understanding of morality in the conduct of their daily lives. They are gullible to the petty economic benefits given by politicians during election time and are willing to sell for few pesos their votes resulting to the increase of corrupt politicians in office. There is a serious need to review  the curriculum adopted by the Department of Education to impart good manners and right conduct among the youth of the land. This is to arrest the serious moral degeneration of the Pilipino race.

 

While it is true that the seeming problem besetting the nation today  is the challenge to the highest leadership of the land because of the charges made against him, the real cause of such problems is morality. Many wants to have a leader that best reflect their own quality of morality. They want a leader that  personify the moral qualities as described above. This is the challenge facing not only the President of the Philippines but also the judges who will exercise their own conscience which will mirror their own personal quality of morality. The whole nation is held in suspense and awaiting the final outcome of the impeachment proceedings, the results of which define the morality of the nation as a whole.

 

Filipino Is A Damaged Race

 

            A politician has said that the political leaders are only reflection of the masses they led. The leaders are just the mirror of the Filipino people  drunks, womanizers and gamblers.  Filipino  is a damaged race. Another CNN international newscaster described the recent kidnapping by Filipinos of foreign tourists in Malaysia and the impeachment of the Philippine President as an indication of a very turbulent country. Many have the appetite for violence, selfishness and greed reflecting the traits of some of their own leaders. But there was a time in not so distant past where the Filipino race is known as  honest, industrious, and dignified. It has a rule of conduct and government of what is known in Philippine history   the Code of Kalantiao followed and respected by all, from the leaders to those they led. The Filipino race is known to be one of the most hospitable race, particularly among strangers. There is also that  Bayanihan spirit among the people in performing task to make the work faster and easier. In fact up to the present time, the Filipino workers overseas are well like by their employers and superiors. Filipinos are loyal, hardworking, and dedicated workers.  One wonders what brought about  the  moral degeneration of the Filipino race?

 

            Some thinkers cited numerous reasons for the degeneration of the moral fibers of the Filipino race. Among the reasons cited are the following:

 

1.                  Lack of true and sincere understanding of good character and moral values. Many leaders in the academe do not appreciate the importance of morally educating the youth. They center the education only on academic matters.  Consequently, there is a noticeable increase in business,  political leaders and  legal luminaries who are intellectual giants but morally dwarf individuals.

.

2.                  Lack of appreciation of the true meanings and essence of the various religions to help improve the character development of man, as citizen of the land and the children of God. Many religious leaders no longer work to help man in the perfection of human soul. They no longer teach what Jesus said Be perfect as your Father in heaven is perfect. These religious leaders concentrate receiving financial and material contribution for the churches using various means. Many followers of various churches are good only while inside the house of God.  They succumbed to evil deeds the moment they step out and perform activities to sustain selfish life.

 

3.                  The elimination of the subject Good manners and right conduct to educate the primary and secondary school children. This subject was taught in  the primary  schools in the 60s and earlier, particularly in the public schools. That time teachers and law enforcers are the most respected public servants.

 

4.                  Too much emphasis in competition and winnings in various academic and sports undertakings. Some leaders and school administrators directly or indirectly support the Machiavilian principle of  the end justify the means. This is at the expense of morality and propriety.

 

 

5.                  The inadequate support of the government in providing the financial requirements of the Department of Education to provide adequate and balance academic, technical, moral, and athletic/sport  training of the Filipino youth.

 

6.                  The underpayment of the teacher services fails to attract those better qualified to teach the school children. This  resulted in  the lower standard of competence and  education of  the youth.

 

7.                  Graft and corruption while officially censored is actually practiced by many public servants and officials. The government bureau task to persecute grafters have not succeeded and weeding out the crime.

 

 

8.                  Respect and recognition are measured by winnings in competition, wealth and power of the leader or individual, less by his high moral and upright qualities.

 

The political and the economic problems besetting the Filipinos will not be addressed by this article. Let the smart  politicians and brilliant economists resolve them. But it will try to address the moral issues  to  rectify and arrest the moral degeneration of the race.

 

The fundamental moral qualities of  man is true to a leader and followers.  Let us describe and understand them.

 

1.                  Kindness and compassionate qualities are very much observable in man and even to brute animals. Such care, kindness and harmlessness qualities are true particularly when they are caring and raring their young ones. Such kindness and harmless can be interpreted as love for their dependents and for the young ones seeking protection from their parents. The parents are the provider of food, shelter, and protection from the predators or enemies roaming around.  The parents only want goodness and better future to their children.  Translated into leading a nation, the leader must exercise compassion and kindness to his constituents, always ready to provide for their needs and protection.  The leader is the protector, not the exploiter of the nation. A morally fit leader serves his constituents well and uses the power of his office to actualize such services.

 

2.                  Tell the truth, not a lie. So simple and so easy said  than done. Those who can actualize this in life can indeed be a leader as he can command respect  from other since they will not hesitate that what he said is untrue and therefore can be relied upon. Anyone caught lying cannot be trusted  and therefore cannot lead for a long time. No follower will  obey  one who lie particularly where life and honor is concern.

 

3.                  Thou shall not steal. This is a moral quality applicable to a follower and to a leader. Stealing is appropriating a thing not legitimately yours and therefore possessing such thing is not right and proper. Someone ask if it is  right  to steal to feed the hungry or to buy medicine to cure the sick? It is correct to serve and provide food for the hungry and buy medicine for the sick, but  not  right  to cause harm to somebody where the goods or money is stolen. One must know the difference between right and wrong, and must exercise proper discrimination of right action at all times. The outward performance of good deeds cannot be made right if  it  results from  the evil or wrong cause. The golden rule of Confucious is the best guideline thus Do not do to other as you do not want other do to you.

 

4.                  Man procreates to provide others opportunity to live and his soul to become perfect. Animals too procreate but only when in heat to bring life to their own kind. And so animals do not suffer sexually transmitted diseases as man do. Animals do not experience the pain of giving birth, as many human beings do. But man has intelligence capable of discrimination and reason of what is right and wrong yet they experiences and suffer sexually transmitted diseases. Animals without the intelligence procreate and obey the laws of nature and so do not suffer the consequences of disease and pain. On the other hand, man of intelligence uses his intellect and creates artificial devices to satisfy lust  thereby violating the laws of nature and the commandment Go and multiply. It is truly shameful for man not to exercise temperance which in spite of intelligence acted lower than animal of instinct. Man therefore suffers various forms of sexually transmitted diseases. The priests and teachers of religions must continue without let-up to remind man to perform  procreation as act of divinity and in accordance with the laws of God and nature.

 

5.                  Man has  knowledge and wisdom to make him uniquely express his own talent and ability as son of God. There is no need to be envy of the talents of others and so there is no need to be covetous, selfish and greedy of others possession.  As he wants to prosper because of own resources and merit so shall he allow others to be so without  being covetous of  others successes. The leader must provide this kind of freedom, recognize the achievement,  and even encourage such qualities if the nation and its  people is to succeed morally and economically.

 

6.                  Man must exercise dicipline and restraints to his own thought, emotion, and action, making sure that others are not  affected  by him in the affairs of daily lives. Just and fair man- made laws of the land must be written for all without exception. The laws must be enforceable and applicable to all from the leaders to the followers.

 

The above mentioned moral qualities may help a long way in the attainment of human regeneration. Such can be taught to the youth,  the masses and the leaders  . Then  no one will say that  Filipino is a damage race or the Philippines a violent country any longer.

 

Idealistic Fervor And  Realistic Goal

 

            There are those who think that knowing and applying good morals is at best idealistic, but in a nation such as ours, we must be realistic and practical to survive and to succeed. Political leaders may have patriotic idealism to rule the country but also have their own selfish interest that often prevail when action and decision are needed. Law enforcers are task to protect the land and its people against criminals but because of selfish interest become the protectors of criminals themselves. Brilliant (but lack of ethical and moral values) lawyers succeeded in the acquittal of crooked businessmen, misguided and immoral politicians by legal technicalities and lack of tangible factual proof of crimes committed. Many citizens think that anyone can commit crime and escape punishment for so long as the crime perpetuator is able to hire  brilliant lawyer to protect   and get him acquitted.  They say that idealistic morality is not applicable and cannot apply to the materialistic race like us.

 

            Evidently, two distinct human traits are the issues one can gather from such observation. First, the realistic or practical traits in man to satisfy material and physical need. Second, the spiritual or idealistic need of man to serve God and fellow human beings. In the absence of high moral values in man, we tend to practice and justify all actions according to the physical and psychological need such as:

 

1.                  desire for material possession and security from wants

2.                  selfishness and greed

3.                  pride for wealth, power and fame

 

The desire for material possession and security from wants arise from the fear of man from hunger, need for shelter, and protection from intrusion on their personal abode and home. The selfishness and greed are the result of fear of sufferings and insecurity in the maintenance of the need of their bodies and want of comfort. The pride for wealth, power and fame are the result of mans need for recognition and respect from other human beings. It is easy to understand that the selfish but practical and realistic man will do anything to secure food, shelter, clothing and other personal properties and possession for himself and his family. And as he develop his intellect and influence his quest for recognition, power and authority also expand to satisfy more his selfishness, greed, and other psychological and material ambition.

 

But man regardless of his position in life has that divine discontent felt within him other than material possession and recognition in life. He can see the injustices happening around him and wish somebody to stop it. He can see graft and corruption happening and that too must stop and the actor and perpetuator punished. He feels that man need a certain kind of morality to direct his thought, feeling, and action in the conduct of life. Unlike animal that is only guided by instinct and act only for own self-protection and   preservation, man has mind and moral values to guide his life. Religions make him aware that he is spiritual  being and has mind to discriminate right and wrong in the conduct of life. He agrees that man lives not by breed alone. He understand that mans life must be full of love, righteousness, compassion and service to fellowmen. The use of intellect to circumvent justice and righteousness must stop and let truth come out and prevail among mankind. Intellect must be used as light to shed out darkness, selfishness, and greed. Intellect must be used to see the beauty of creation of God and as tool to serve mankind. He must work not only to save him from damnation but more to make his soul perfect as God the Father is perfect. Right values and action is the first step towards this direction. One therefore is encouraged to adopt idealistic way of life as oppose to purely materialistic way of life.

 

The two opposing traits in man must now come to a head. One is to choose which of such traits he needs to develop and to practice in life. In fact the selection of a leader is guided by  the moral traits of man as described below:

 

One is motivated to lead because of the following:

 

1.      Name and fame

2        Wealth and power

3        Selfish interest

4        Combination of the above l, 2, and 3

5        Selfless interest

 

For want of name and fame, one is motivated to lead an organization, a society, and even a nation. He will use his own financial resources and intellect to be elected in his chosen field and interest and be acknowledged by its members and constituents.

 

For want of wealth and power expected to be realized and earned, one is motivated to join an organization, a society, and/or a political party  and eventually worked to be their leader for the ultimate goal of  earning wealth and power. He will work and develop strategy and scheme to realize economic and financial gain for being the leader.

 

For selfish objective(s) realizable only if he belongs to an organization, a political party, and any society and group, one will aspire for a leadership position in the profession where be belongs or even in a political party where he can be chosen and lead. Then he will use the corresponding power of his position for selfish goal and interest.

 

For want of the first three above-mentioned motivation and interest, one will work hard to attain and to realize the leadership position. He will do everything workable (legal or illegal; or moral and immoral) scheme and strategy to realize his ambition and will execute them at whatever costs it take. He will be willing to spend a fortune to realize his ambition, bribe constituents to get him elected, compromise and engage in horse-trading to stay course with his leadership ambition and plans. He will do whatever it takes against those who will oppose his goal and plan. Anyone can see these type of leaders from among the owners of industries and political leaders in government.

 

For the great love of mankind and God and to serve his own kind,  his nation, and all beings in general, the late Mother Theresa is a good example of the selfless leader that the 5th type of leader is. The sages and saints, the military generals and soldiers who died protecting the nation are good example of selfless type of men. Their motivation is to serve mankind in general and the nation in particular. They do not work to enrich themselves putting control on selfish ambition and interest. Many of our underpaid teachers whose interest is to educate our youth and bring about future leaders of our nation are equally of this type. Also those different religious leaders who work hard for the perfection of the human souls disregarding selfish interest and risk of  their lives are also of this type. And of course there are political, civic and business leaders past and present who also belong to this type.

 

Your choice of a leader defines your morality in life.
