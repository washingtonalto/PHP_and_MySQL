MORALITY: A  SERIOUS PROBLEM OF THE NATION

( Part II )

Jesse D. Alto

 

A television host once asked “ Is Filipino still worth dying for? There are many who responded yes or no and the reason why to the question to justify the answer. Another friend told me apparently as a joke that these days, crime pays considering that many wealthy and politically powerful persons enjoy political, economic comfort, power and perceived respectability from the society primarily due to the immoral ways they acquire wealth, power and fame. Many of these crooks are not persecuted and brought to the hall of justice since they use money and power to intimidate and silence the few who dare to expose their wrongdoings. I once heard that a crooked but very wealthy businessman once asked a ranking government official how much is she worth to drop the case of tax delinquency now in court. When she refused, the case is continued to be challenged in court by the businessman who hires  talented lawyers  with help from some powerful politicians to delay if not destroy the case once and for all. He boasted that it is cheaper to pay legal fees and bribe some legal luminaries and politicians than to pay taxes correctly.

 

The kidnapping incidents all over the country and proliferation of the illegal drug business that destroy man are a sign that the moral fibers of those engage in it are low. They are primarily motivated to serve selfish interest at the expense of the nation as a whole. Worse, there is perception and allegation that some of those who are task to stop the kidnappers and drug pushers are equally guilty of participation in the crime by allowing the kidnappers escape and drug pushers elude conviction. There are also allegations that some ransom money finds its way to the law enforcers who are task to stop the crime. Ask your friend overseas and if you have a chance to travel abroad experience it yourself and find out  how bad the image the country is hurting the Filipino overseas. Consequently less foreign investment flows into the country, and the tourism industry is severely affected because of the kidnapping incidents. These are now hurting the nation in general.

 

A few who dare to expose or to confess their participations to the crimes mentioned above are being discredited and gang up by those politicians and  law enforcers who are afraid that they may later on be exposed of yet unknown and undetected wrongdoings. Much time and energy of lawmakers are being spent  to discredit those who expose the crimes than establishing the commission of the crime and creating new laws to prevent its recurrence and persecuting those who committed the crimes itself. Some say that if we can only implement and enforce half of the existing laws of the land we will be much better-off   economically, politically and morally than ever before. They must be reminded that if there is smoke there is fire.  Or when asked what to do to the sinner Mary Magdalene, Lord Jesus said. “ He who is pure and sinless of the sin charged on her cast the first stone.” None of those present dare to stone her. Some of our selfish leaders want to make laws as a show of  power, never serious and even afraid of implementing them.

 

ATTRACTION OF WEALTH, OF POWER, AND FAME

 

            Everyone, in varying degree of intensity and attachment, is attracted to wealth, power and fame. Everyone wants money to secure for his bodily need and comfort. Everyone needs power to promote self-steem and importance.  Everyone wants fame and honor as sign of recognition and superiority among his peers. All these he wants as expression of happiness and freedom. Happiness is a joy of living, an expression of his presence in this physical world and using all the means of money, power and fame can give. Freedom is the power to create and actualize that which he thinks necessary for his own well-being. As the world daily  rotates around the sun experiencing day and night, sunny, rainy, snowy and misty, so is man experiences happiness and pain  in spite of wealth, power and fame. The more he attaches himself in wealth, power, and fame, the more he becomes selfish and fearful of losing his possession. Then all means are resorted to retain them and so crime is committed to perpetuate his wealth, power and fame. He becomes the center of his world. Anybody that crosses his path is a threat and must be crushed. He becomes selfish  and  will cheat,  kill, lie, and do anything to protect selfish interest. He is engrossed and so attached to what the physical world can give  and has forgotten what his religious faith and spiritual nature about him is all about. Evidently, many become selfish and seek wealth, power, and fame in their quest for the elusive and everlasting happiness. What they get is temporary and illusory pleasure followed sooner or later by excruciating pain. Science explains it as the law of cause and effect, or in nature, the law of compensation. Lord Jesus said, “What you sow, you reap.” One who sow selfishness and greed the effect of which cause pain to others, gets back sooner or later its painful result.  Needless to say, one can cite innumerable examples of this principle among the disgraced ordinary men and leaders of mankind.

 

MORALITY GUIDE TO TRUE HAPPINESS

 

            An ancient sage defines man as spirit and matter link by intelligence. The spiritual man uses his material (physical, vital, and emotional) bodies and intelligence towards happiness and perfection. The ultimate sanction of morality is the spiritual man’s desire for happiness in his true home and the escape from misery from this transitory place called the world.  John Stuart Mill defines happiness as the state of absence of opposition. An ancient sage defines happiness in more forcible terms: Every kind of subjugation to another is pain and subjugation to one’s self is happiness. The whole system of nature is moving in a particular direction, and this direction is determined by the composition of two forces, namely, the one acting from the pole of existence towards ordinarily called matter and other pole called spirit moving the opposite direction. The very fact that nature is moving shows that these two forces are not equal in magnitude The forces directed towards spirit is the one which must, though not without hard struggle, ultimately prevail. This is the great directing energy of nature.  If an individual attempts to move in a direction other than that in which nature is moving that individual is sure to be crushed. The only way which happiness might be attained is by merging one’s nature with the great mother’s nature and following the direction in which she herself is moving.

 

            Simply stated, understanding the following is a guide to high ethical and moral standard which bring permanent happiness to the spiritual man:

 

1.      Man is a spiritual being clothe by physical, vital, emotional and mental bodies.

2.      He uses intelligence to experience in the world of forms and matters.

3.      At the present time, like the prodigal son, he forgets his true spiritual nature and identified himself with his lower bodies thus he becomes self centered and selfish. He is subjugated and imprisoned by his lower bodies. His spiritual nature must subjugate his lower bodies to experience lasting happiness.

4.      Like the prodigal son, the spiritual man is yet to realize he is eating foods fit for the swine. At the present time man is so much attach to material wealth, power and fame which are temporary in nature and will not last long without corresponding pain to the beholder.

5.      The prevailing crimes and wrongdoings committed by men are due primarily to their attachment to wealth, power, and fame and the erroneous understanding that such are the givers of happiness. Such wealth, power, and fame may provide pleasure and temporary happiness to the beholder followed by more excruciating pain sooner or later especially if those are acquired by illegal or immoral means. This will provide man the lesson needed living in this world and to awake him to his true existence and understanding his true spiritual nature. Love and service are antidote to greed and selfishness.

6.      Man must understand and cooperate with the laws of nature and not against her. He will then gain right knowledge necessary for his own spiritual perfection, the re-building of King Solomon’s temple not made by hand.

7.      Harmlessness to all beings, truth, honesty, purity, and surrender to God are some of the qualities of man with high ethical and moral standards.

 

EDUCATION

 

            The main trust of the present educational system is primarily to train and develop the youth learn the necessary skills to make them economically productive in their adult life. The youth are also encouraged to become achievers in sport and intellectual undertaking making many to become extremely self centered and individualistic. In some cases achievements are reached through dishonestly and at all costs. In other words the goal to educate the youth is materialistic and selfish. There is less attention to  let the youth understand  on the purposes of life and the wholesome art of living. Therefore, to reverse the enhancement of selfishness and greed among the youth, the educational system must incorporate lessons on cooperation, tolerance, love, harmony and these are through the teachings of  good manners and right conduct. Living in harmony and cooperation must prevail instead of cut-throat competition. Contest is a measure of how one develops his physical or mental faculties and not a measure of superiority among his peers. The man’s development of greater physical and mental faculties is indicative of greater service to fellow men. The true essence of religious teachings may likewise be taught to strengthen the moral fibers of the youth.

 

ENRICHMENT OF FAITH (Religious teachings)

 

            There are various religious teachings available easily in the churches or temples of our faith for study, use, and practice.  They are teachings design to regenerate mankind to enable him to understand his true nature and to live in peace and happiness without any trace of greed and selfishness. For example,  Christianity encourages his followers to be ever devoted and faithful to Christ. Among the Christian teachings is the self surrender of one and devotedness of man to God.  If every Christian will follows the teachings of the spiritual Christ, all Christian will practice universal brotherhood of men and there will be love, tolerance and peace throughout the world. After all in our prayer we assert that we are imperfect children of a perfect Father. The bible is clear asking imperfect spiritual man to be perfect as our God the  Father which is in heaven is perfect.  Buddhism teaches harmlessness and love to all beings among others. Self reliant and own efforts towards illumination or nirvana are important Buddhist teachings.  Islam and Hinduism are religious teachings primarily teaches man the oneness of all beings. Zoroastrian religious teachings encourage man to purify his  thoughts, words and deeds.

 

            Through understanding, an open mind, and tolerance, the conflict and difficulty in the various seemingly different teachings of religions can be avoided. One must realize that in the long history of mankind, different religious teachings were introduced not to supersede one by the other but to expand the teachings to make man good all around.  The Great Ones introduce religious teachings most appropriate for the time, the  place and people ready for such teachings.  One will notice that the quality of man’s character and values are enhanced by such teachings. All the religious teachings are design to make man godlike  and to realize his true spiritual nature and perfection as said by Jesus Christ that all must work to be perfect as our Father in heaven is perfect. It is clear that the perfection mentioned by Lord Jesus Christ is spiritual perfection and not material perfection as no one can be perfect in one mortal life. The Buddhist teaching is for one to reach illumination or nirvana, meaning his true and pure spiritual nature free from gunas or material forms and its qualities and imprisoning attributes. Islam and Hinduism teachings lead one to the final realization of oneness with God in the spiritual plane and not in this lower world which we call matter. Man’s existence in the material or physical world enable him to experience and to live in the world of matter and form necessary to his spiritual growth and evolutionary progress.

 

            The various religious teachings may vary in point, in emphasis and in part but in essence all religious teachings are given to mankind to make them perfect, reach nirvana or illumination, or realize their unity with God. Great wars and religious conflicts happened in the past and a few conflicts are still happening at the present time not because one religion is better or superior than the other or God which is all good, all loving, and most merciful has created inferior religion to lead some of his children to eternal damnation but because of the emphasis given on differences and methods of teachings by some religious teachers at the time. Even in the ordinary human family life, no sensible father will teach his children evil and wrong things in life. Children may have different interest and go separate ways towards perfection in life but their father will always be supporting them. Different religious teachings are given to support and enhance the different temperaments of men. Every one is therefore encourage to enhance and to enrich his understanding of the deeper meanings of his faith, learning to extract the meat from the husk and live with it.

 

            Morality is the foundation of peace and happiness of mankind. Morality is the guiding tools that man may live happily and in unity in the diverse world where all are expressing unique creative qualities of mankind. To live to serve those who are yet less creative and in want is the greatest goal of  those fortunate men who are more perfect in the art of creation. To live by the golden rule and to serve mankind is the greatest gift of man to his Creator.
