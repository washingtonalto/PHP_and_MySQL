TERRORISM

                                                            Jesse D. Alto

 

 

            On September 11, 2001 starting 8:45 a.m. New York time and onward for almost a day, people by the hundreds of millions   watched on televisions worldwide or actual eye witnesses to the most horrific terrorism unfolding in modern times and against the most militarily and economically powerful nation by slamming two hijacked commercial airplanes into the buildings bringing down like houses of glass the twin ll0 floors tallest buildings in the world. In addition, two lower buildings within the area also collapsed and more buildings are damaged in danger of collapsing.  A third hijacked commercial plane was slammed into a military building in Pentagon, Washington, D.C., the military main headquarters of the United States of America. A fourth hijacked commercial plane missed its supposed target and slammed in forested area in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, U.S.A.  Hundreds firefighters and police officers perished while trying to save lives and put off the fires. Thousands died and billions of dollars lost from such act of terrorism. The attacks of the Japanese military forces during the Second World War on the American military forces at Pearl Harbor created also such terrors to the Americans and united them and resolved which they did to win the war. But the recent terrorism that destroyed the World Trade Center twin towers and the partial destruction of building in the  Pentagon and killed thousands unarmed civilians is considered more horrific, cruel and violent than the one at Pearl Harbor.

 

            The United States of America considered such act of terrorism as an act of war and they resolved to bring to justice and will retaliate in whatever means against the perpetrators of the crimes and those that harbor them.

 

            Not comparable in scope and magnitude in relation to the above-mentioned acts of terrorisms in New York and Washington, DC and yet still acts of terror are those kidnapping incidents happening in our midst. Terrorists and kidnappers who took victims for ransoms are affecting the tourist industries and preventing investors to come and do business in the Philippines. Kidnapping instill fear from the peaceful and non-violent people and more so to the victims. Some political/religious groups to create chaos for political and/or economic gain commit it and such creates political instability and economic uncertainty to the nation.

 

WHAT IS TERRORISM

 

            Terror is an intense, overpowering fear. It is object, occurrence and anything that instill such fear. It is violence toward private citizens, public property, and political enemies promoted by a political group to achieve or maintain supremacy. It may be an annoying or intolerable pest and nuisance. Terrorism is the use of terror, violence and intimidation to achieve an end.

 

WHO COMMIT TERRORIST ACTS

 

            Man is the being that commits most of the acts of terror. While some animals show their strength and power to defend foods, territory and domain, those powerful animals seldom if at all commit violence to instill fear among its kind. But man in spite of his supposedly superior mind and intelligence is capable to commit terrorism worse than that of animal.

 

 TERRORIST ACTS

 

            The acts of terror were practiced and used by many since the ancient times.  Often the strong are the ones who used it against the weak or the defeated. For example, in war, the victors get the spoil. The weak too use terrors to harass and to destabilize the government in power. This they do for economic benefits later or for the expected political fortune and conversion of faith of those defeated when those in power fail and fall. There are leaders so frustrated in the coming of justice for long perceived injustices that they go to hill and execute justice themselves. In some cases politicians use terrorism to get elected and perpetuate themselves in power. Political position is power hard to give up like wine that intoxicates the drinker. The aim to serve his constituents is replaced by the thirst for power, money and fame. In some distant past, there are some religious leaders and writers who appeared to have revised and twisted the meanings of some of the holy teachings of their faith to convert new followers into their fold and keep them within controls. They instill fear of eternal damnation and levy sanctions against disbelievers and violators. Contribution and blind faith are demanded from (gullible?) followers in exchange for blessings and grace to assure seat in heaven come judgment time. As if God can be bribed and bought out (from His own material creation), they ask material contribution for spiritual salvation. The act of contributing material thing or money is charitable act of service and compassion but becomes a bribe as an exchange for grace, a passport to heaven. Is not charity an act of service by fellow human being   without expectation of returns of whatever kind?  The demand by religious leaders to donate money or kind may mean a very subtle form of terrorism too since it seems that man’s spiritual perfection and salvation can be assured in exchange for material contribution. It follows that man can continue to commit crimes and wrongdoings against all beings in general and mankind in particular and still receive grace and forgiveness so long as he follows blindly his religious leaders and give material contribution in exchange for grace and forgiveness of repeatedly committing wrongdoings. Blind faith to a charismatic religious leader may led man to kill fellow human beings and still thinks that God agrees to the killing which is his passport to eternal bliss and salvation. Religious teachings are mixed up with political motives to serve selfish end.

 

Indeed there is an urgent need to study and understand true religious teachings designed to make man good all around. The foundation of all religions is to make man good all around. Zoroastrian taught purity in thoughts, words, and deeds; love and harmlessness to all beings by Buddhism; unity of all by Hinduism and Islam; and devotion and self surrender by Christianity are clear examples that all religions are taught by the Great Ones at different times and place to suit the temperaments and the people to make man good all around. Other religions taught men their true spiritual nature and their potentialities toward reaching perfection. As time went on, the succeeding religious leaders and writers of holy books knowingly or otherwise may have twisted the meanings of some of the teachings that created frictions and conflicts within their faith and that of other faiths. Others concentrate on the minor interpretation of differences that widen conflicts and created disunity and disharmony leading to the formation of different religious sects. For example, is it normal for a human father to ask one son to kill another disobedient son as sign of love and devotion to him? Certainly no father will ask such wrongdoing more so to tolerate any son to commit such crime. Can the God, our Father consent His children, mankind that is, to kill one another just because of different faiths and they express kind of temperaments and the different ways they developed in themselves goodness, unity, perfection and love to their Father? Certainly NO. Then why do we resort to violence to prove a point and assert our faith on others like what happened in the religious wars during the middle age and some others in more distant past? Then why do we quarrel when we interpret differently the teachings of the Great Ones?  Certainly the holy books recording the teachings of the Great Ones must be good and helpful to mankind. But it may be possible that some writers in some very distant past may have twisted some meanings changing the essence of the original teachings that created confusion and conflicts. The changes in meanings polarize the faith of men and resulted to wars, killings, and other destructive consequences. Some people observed that the destruction of World Trade Center buildings and the Pentagon in the United States of America, the continuing war in the middle East and the kidnappings and conflicts between the Muslim and the Christian Filipinos in the Philippines are the results of  the on going religious/political conflicts.

 

MAN IS GOOD

 

            An ancient sage said that man is spirit and matter link by intelligence. The illumination, realization of unity, purity and perfection of man is reached when he realized his spirituality, not materiality and thread the path to holiness as directed by the religion and true teachings of his faith and by the Great Ones.  Man is like a child holding a lighted lamp, his intelligence. That light can burn as well as guide him in his spiritual quest to unity, illumination and perfection. Improperly used, that led him to pride, greed and selfishness. Properly used that led him to service, love, purity, tolerance, understanding and selflessness. Let our religious leaders of the world by correct and good examples and pure teachings develop true spiritual nature of men.

             

            The civilized nations and its people today are capable resolving religious conflicts and differences in civilized and contrary to the barbaric manners as happened many times in the past. The death tolls of such conflicts in the middle age religious wars and the current ones are countless and senseless. It is also ironic and contrary to the essence of love that the children of one loving and compassionate God are willing to kill others to show love and devotion and unity with Him. Long ignored because of indifference and pride and touches the emotional and mental faculties of some of the religious leaders, it may still be prudent that among their scholars and theologians and religious luminaries may form study of the similarities and differences of the teachings of all religions with complete honesty and openness. These scholars may have to research old and long forgotten records in archives and libraries of government and religious institutions. The punishments for political and criminal acts are to be separated from the problems pose by religious differences in understanding and application of faith. The similarities of the teachings will strengthen our faith in one God and the differences will enrich man intellectual faculties and understanding and broaden his love and compassion very necessary in his quest for unity and perfection. They may share the results of the study with their followers and learn and practice understanding and tolerance from differences noted. It may be prudent to avoid proselytizing someone else. This will promote cooperation, harmony and peace and avoid senseless conflict and killings in the name of religion. Men as children of God should not repeat the errors in the past when they engaged in wars and cruel and merciless killings of fellow human beings. Harmlessness, truth, tolerance, purity and understanding of the faith of others will promote harmony and cooperation and altogether when spice with love will eliminate terrorism.
